# Content Playbook — methodology

Reusable, channel-agnostic tactics your agent uses when drafting and reviewing. Unlike the
other reference files, this one ships **filled in** — it's craft, not your specific brand.
Adapt the channel-specific notes to where you post; edit freely as you learn what works.

## Cadence & mix
- **Consistency beats volume.** Pick a rate you can sustain (see `content-roadmap.md`) and hold it.
- **Balance the mix** across your ranked goals. Don't run two hard CTAs of the same type
  back-to-back — space product/offer asks out with authority and story posts.

## Anatomy of a post
1. **Hook (line 1):** its only job is to earn line 2. Most feeds truncate after a couple of lines —
   the hook has to land before "see more."
2. **Whitespace:** short lines, single-sentence paragraphs. Scannable on a phone.
3. **Body:** one idea. A story, a specific example, a real artifact, or a contrarian take. Show the work.
4. **CTA (one only):** matched to the goal. Convert → buy/signup/DM. Grow → subscribe/follow.
   Authority → soft or none.
5. **Hashtags / tags:** where the channel uses them, keep them few and specific. Skip on channels
   that don't reward them.
6. **Links:** on channels that suppress reach for outbound links in the body (e.g. some social
   feeds), put the link in the first comment or a follow-up instead.

## Hook formulas (rotate — don't reuse the same one twice in a week)
- **Contrarian:** "Most advice about X is wrong. Here's what actually works."
- **Number / result:** "I did X in 3 days. Here's the exact process."
- **Story cold-open:** "In 2019 I made a bet that nearly broke the company. Here's what it taught me."
- **Problem-agitate:** "Your [thing] is built on the loudest voice in the room, not the most common need."
- **Callout to the reader:** "If you're a [persona] stuck on [pain] — read this."
- **Behind-the-scenes:** "Here's exactly how I [do the thing] — the real version, not the demo."
- **Myth-bust:** "'[Common belief]' misses what's actually happening. Here's why."

## Post formats (pick one per draft)
- **The teardown** — break down how you built/solved something, step by step. *(educate, authority)*
- **The thesis** — a strong point of view argued in a few tight lines. *(authority, reach)*
- **The lesson** — a story → one transferable takeaway. *(trust)*
- **The build log** — "This week I shipped X. Here's what worked / broke." *(authority, consistency)*
- **The product moment** — a real problem → how your offer addresses it → one CTA. *(convert / grow)*
- **The invitation** — direct: "Here's who I help and how. Want in?" *(convert — use sparingly)*

## Converting content → your goal (the funnel)
1. **Authority + educate** posts prove you know the thing → warm the audience.
2. **Proof + point-of-view** posts make the offer legible → "oh, this is what they do."
3. **Invitation + product** posts + comment/DM engagement → the conversion.
4. **In the DMs/comments:** be helpful first, qualify gently, then move to the next step. Don't
   hard-sell in public.
- **Make the destination match:** profile/bio and link should reflect the offer before you drive
  traffic. Flag it if it doesn't.

## Converting content → audience growth
- Lead with the *problem*, not the product — the person nodding at the problem self-selects.
- Make the ask low-friction: "comment a word / DM me / subscribe" beats "fill out this form."
- Follow up fast; ask early adopters for feedback (it feeds future proof and build-log posts).

## Engagement tactics
- Reply to every comment in the first 1-2 hours (most algorithms + the relationship reward it).
- Comment thoughtfully on your audience's posts a few times a day — visibility compounds.
- Repurpose winners: a strong post → a carousel/thread → a longer article → a reply template.
- Post when your audience is actually on — test a couple of windows and keep what works.

## Sound human, not AI (anti-slop — run on EVERY draft)
AI-flagged posts get less reach and less engagement, and savvy audiences clock slop instantly.
Before anything ships:
- **Minimize em-dashes (—).** They've become the #1 AI tell. Prefer periods, commas, or parentheses.
  Aim for zero; one per post, max.
- **Break cadence uniformity.** Vary sentence length hard — mix 3-word fragments with longer lines.
  If every sentence runs ~15-20 words, it reads like a bot.
- **Cut the AI-blacklist words:** delve, tapestry, leverage, testament, seamless, robust, elevate,
  unlock, game-changer, "in today's world," "it's not just X, it's Y," "furthermore/moreover."
- **Add one real, specific detail** only the author knows — a number, a name, a moment, a thing that
  shipped. Specificity is the strongest humanizer. If a draft has a `[BRACKET]` for this, it's not
  done until it's filled.
- **Hand-edit the first and last line.** They carry the most human signal.
- **Read it out loud.** Any sentence the author wouldn't actually say, rewrite. Catches the most slop.
- **Start from a real take,** not a blank prompt — a messy human opinion first, then sharpen.

## Measuring
- Track per post in `content-log.md`: reach/impressions, comments, DMs, and — most important — the
  metric that matters for the goal (signups, sales, subscribers, calls booked).
- Double down on the pillars/formats that produce the real goal, not just likes.
